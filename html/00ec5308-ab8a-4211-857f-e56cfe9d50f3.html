Relevance Feedback

Relevance feedback is a feature of some information retrieval systems. The idea behind relevance feedback is to take the results that are initially returned from a given query and to use information about whether or not those results are relevant to perform a new query. The user gives feedback on the relevance of documents in an initial set of results. The basic procedure is to start with an initial query, retrieve documents, have the user mark documents as relevant or not relevant, and then modify the query and repeat. Relevance feedback is most useful in systems where the user is unsure of what they are looking for or how to express their information need.
