Precision and Recall

In pattern recognition, information retrieval and classification, precision is the fraction of relevant instances among the retrieved instances, while recall is the fraction of relevant instances that were retrieved. Both precision and recall are therefore based on relevance. Precision can be seen as a measure of quality, and recall as a measure of quantity. Higher precision means that an algorithm returns more relevant results than irrelevant ones. Higher recall means that an algorithm returns most of the relevant results. In information retrieval contexts, precision and recall are used to evaluate the performance of search engines and other retrieval systems.
